Gameplay

Explore core mechanics of Deserts of Kharak, and its unique features inspired by previous titles of the franchise

Core Mechanics

  • Resource Gathering - Salvagers collect Construction Units (CU) and Research Units (RU) from wrecks and crash sites.
  • Unit Roles - Rock paper scissors system with LAVs, armor units, artillery, railguns, and air units.
  • Carrier as Base - Your carrier acts as your main production hub and ultimate weapon.
  • Sensors Manager - Tactical top-down view for issuing orders and monitoring enemy movements.
  • Story-Driven Campaign - Varied mission objectives and cinematic storytelling.

Unique to Deserts of Kharak

  • Terrain Elevation & Line-of-Sight - Hills, dunes and ridges block fire, high ground increases range and visibility.
  • Armored Facing - Units take different damage depending on whether hit from the front, side, or rear.
  • Mobile Combat Carriers - Fully armed carriers with upgradable weapons, armor, and systems.
  • Power Management - Divert power between weapons, armor, sensors, or speed mid-battle.
  • Dune Skimming - Units gain speed bonuses on certain desert terrain types.

Power Management

Power Management allows you to redistribute the carrier's available power between systems such as weapons, shields, and engines for tactical advantage.

Choosing which to distribute the power to, will depend on the situation. Effective power allocation will help last longer against your opponents

Terrain Line of Sight

Line of Sight determines unit visibility on the battlefield. Units cannot detect enemies hidden behind terrain or outside their sensor range.

Terrain Elevation

Terrain Elevation affects weapon range, accuracy, and visibility. Higher ground offers tactical advantages over lower positions.

Units positioned on elevated terrain can shoot farther and deal increase damage improve line of sight, making control of high ground a tactical objective
